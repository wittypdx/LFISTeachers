Bank

You will need to get an American bank account set up as soon as you arrive so you can receive a checkbook and a Debit card. Here are some popular banks in Oregon:

  • Chase
  • Bank of the West
  • Wells Fargo
  • Bank of America

You do not need to make an appointment, just go into whatever bank you chose and ask to set up a checking account (and a savings account if you want). The biggest difference between banks and the thing to look out for are hidden fees. Some banks charge a monthly fee just for having an account there, others will charge you $10 every time you deposit a check, some will fine you if the amount of money in your account drops below a certain point. Banks will also often have financial advisors who can provide advice for free. For example there are account options where you can earn a higher monthly interest. Be careful when considering these options though because often they involve putting a large sum of money in an account and agreeing not to touch that money for 2-3 years (for CDs) to 30+ years (for IRAs and retirement accounts). If you feel you need some support while setting up an account Danielle, Seema, Alice, Elise or any of the teachers can help you out.

IDs and social security cards

These two things are only loosely related to banks but if you decide to stay in the states for a long period of time you should consider getting a social security card and a US ID card.

You will find very quickly that you need a social security number to do pretty much anything (taxes, most rental application, background checks, etc). As a non-US citizen you will need:

  • Your passport (with your visa)
  • You I-551, I-94, or I-766 form
  • Your original birth certificate
  • Your work contract with L'Etoile
  • A completed application

Once you have these documents together you can go to the Social Security Offices in Portland and they will complete your application or let you know what additional documents you need.

After you get your social security number you can apply for an Oregon ID card or even get your driver's license.
